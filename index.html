Why we exist:

Fascism has invaded our community. In 2016, St. Patrick's Church in Hudson was asked to take in five families of Muslim Syrian refugees. Fleeing their war torn homeland, they sought refuge in Hudson because they had other family living close by. The Citizens for the St. Croix Valley, from here on labled CSCV, strongly opposed giving asylum. Dianne Joachim of New Richmond made a case to the St. Croix County board that letting in these refugees would spread disease. In an act going against the teachings of Jesus, the Golden Rule, and a basic human kindness, Joachim claimed that letting Muslims in was too risky for their Hudson community. She said they should just stay in Syria, in the midst of a civil war, where they belong.
